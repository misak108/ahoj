Discover the Best Dishes and Restaurants in Rome, Florence, and Beyond

Italy is a dream destination for food lovers. From pizza in Naples to pasta in Florence, Italy’s culinary delights are endless. In this guide, we explore the must-try dishes and the best restaurants in Italy’s most famous cities: Rome, Florence, and beyond.

1. Rome: The Heart of Italian Cuisine

Rome is famous for its rich and comforting dishes. Here are a few classic Roman dishes that you can't miss:

  • Carbonara: A Roman classic made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. It's rich and delicious!
  • Cacio e Pepe: A simple yet flavorful pasta made with Pecorino Romano cheese and black pepper. A true Roman comfort dish.
  • Supplì: Fried rice balls filled with mozzarella and tomato sauce, a perfect snack or appetizer.

For the best food in Rome, check out these top restaurants:

  • Roscioli: A famous delicatessen, bakery, and restaurant offering traditional Roman dishes with a modern twist.
  • Trattoria Monti: A beloved spot for traditional Roman pasta dishes and homemade specialties.
  • Piperno: A family-owned restaurant in the Jewish Ghetto, known for its classic Roman Jewish cuisine.

2. Florence: The City of Art and Food

Florence is known for its incredible art and equally impressive food. Tuscan cuisine is hearty and rich in flavor. Must-try dishes include:

  • Bistecca alla Fiorentina: A large, T-bone steak grilled to perfection, typically served rare and seasoned with olive oil, garlic, and rosemary.
  • Pappa al Pomodoro: A traditional tomato and bread soup, perfect for a light but flavorful meal.
  • Ribollita: A thick, vegetable and bean soup that is a staple of Tuscan cuisine, often served with crusty bread.

For dining in Florence, these restaurants are highly recommended:

  • Osteria All’Antico Vinaio: Known for its delicious and affordable sandwiches, this is a must-visit for street food lovers.
  • Trattoria Sostanza: A classic Florentine eatery offering traditional dishes like Bistecca alla Fiorentina and ribollita.
  • Enoteca Pinchiorri: A Michelin-starred restaurant offering refined Tuscan cuisine and an extensive wine list.

3. Naples: Pizza Paradise

No food guide to Italy would be complete without mentioning Naples, the birthplace of pizza. Neapolitan pizza is world-renowned for its thin, soft crust, fresh ingredients, and delicious simplicity. Some must-try pizza spots include:

  • Da Michele: A historic pizzeria famous for its classic Margherita and Marinara pizzas.
  • Gino Sorbillo: One of Naples' most popular pizzerias, known for its fresh ingredients and perfectly baked pizza.
  • Pizzeria Di Matteo: Offering pizza in the heart of Naples' historic center, it’s a local favorite.

4. Venice: Seafood and Risotto

Venice is known for its seafood and risotto dishes, thanks to its coastal location. Must-try dishes include:

  • Risotto al Nero di Seppia: A risotto made with cuttlefish ink, giving it a rich, dark color and unique flavor.
  • Fritto Misto: A fried seafood platter that features a variety of fresh, lightly battered fish and shellfish.
  • Bigoli in Salsa: A traditional Venetian pasta dish made with long, thick noodles and a savory anchovy sauce.

Venice also boasts some incredible dining options:

  • Antiche Carampane: A cozy and romantic restaurant offering Venetian specialties like risotto and fresh seafood.
  • Osteria alle Testiere: Known for its fresh seafood, this intimate eatery offers a perfect Venetian dining experience.
  • Cipriani: One of Venice’s most iconic fine-dining restaurants, offering classic Italian dishes and an amazing view of the lagoon.
